\section*{III. Western Philosophy: Freedom, Will, and the Structure of Fulfillment}

an experiment in self-disclosure: when desire is granted its object, what is revealed about
the freedom that desired it? From Schopenhauer's diagnosis of will as insatiable lack through
existentialist accounts of project and despair to phenomenological analyses of finitude and the
other, and finally to Hegel's dialectic of recognition, each thinker supplies a distinct optic
through which ``getting what one wants'' becomes punitiveâ€”not because satisfaction is withheld,
but because it exposes a structure (metaphysical, existential, ethical) that the will
